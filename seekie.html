Seekie : A Crawler Robot

So, I wanted a remote-controlled vehicle to play with. A car? Eh, they're everywhere. A drone? Nah, too pricey (Well... Seekie's metal chassis was kinda expensive, but not *that* much). A tank? Now we're talking! But not just any tank – a blue one! And that's how Seekie came to life. (Seekie comes from "seeker" 'cause it can move around and "seek" things!) Wait, though... how am I gonna control this thing? I don’t have a remote, and I have no idea how to make one either. But hold up... don’t I have a phone? And I do know how to make an app! Yep! that’s right! And that’s how the Seekie app was born. I can control Seekie from my phone – go forward, backward, left, right, see through its eyes [camera], and even make them blink [headlights]. Seekie can’t drive himself yet, but with his camera and ToF sensors, maibe he soon will!

Seekie life timeline

"Seekie's chassis arrives!" – The foundation of Seekie, fresh from its shipment, marking the beginning of its journey.

"Powering up!" – The motor driver and batteries are integrated, breathing life into Seekie's movement.

"Brains onboard!" – A Raspberry Pi 3 is mounted onto the chassis, laying the groundwork for intelligence and control.

"Vision activated!" – With the Raspberry Pi and a camera installed, Seekie is now equipped with vision and full power connectivity.

"Lighting the way!" – Seekie shines in the dark with its headlights, ready for night-time exploration.

"Seekie functional!" – Now fully app-controlled, Seekie navigates the world with precision.

Seekie's App

The Seekie app is a simple yet powerful tool that lets you control Seekie right from your phone. You can move Seekie forward, backward, left, and right, and most importantly control the tracks independently at any speed you want! You can also see through Seekie's eyes using the camera and even make its headlights blink. Designed to be user-friendly and intuitive, the app lets you take full control of Seekie with ease.
